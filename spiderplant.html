How to Care for a Spider Plant

By Morgyn Peay



Buy $49, bloomscape.com

Spider plants are handsome in any décor. The long - grassy leaves typically are striped white and green or yellow. It takes its name from the baby plants, which resemble clusters of spiders dangling from long stems.
Native to Africa, the plant was first introduced to Europe at the end of the 18th century and was a mainstay in Victorian parlors. It's likely your grandma and mom had one, too. It is still very popular today.

Plant Attributes

  • Common Name: Spider Plant

  • Botonical Name: Chlorophytum comosum

  • Plant Type: Herbaceous perennial housepant

  • Sun Exposure: Part sun or shade

  • Mature Size: 12-24 in. tall and can spread to 12-36 in.

  • Soil Type: Loose nutrient rich potting soil

Plant Care

During initial growth, water occasionally; once fully developed (within one year), water moderately. In the spring and summer months, keep the soil moist to encourage growth. Do not let the soil dry out too much and maintain average room temperature and humidity. Spider plants prefer temperatures between 55 and 80°F, which makes them a great indoor houseplant. Fertilize up to twice a month in the spring and summer, however, avoid overfertilization.
